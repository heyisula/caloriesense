Cold/Rainy conditions trigger thermogenesis so body burns extra calories to maintain core temperature (37°C). Research by van Marken Lichtenbelt et al. (New England Journal of Medicine, 2009) confirmed cold exposure measurably increases metabolic rate. Studies on cold-weather running consistently show a 5–10% increase in calorie expenditure.

Hot/Sunny conditions increase cardiovascular strain. Your heart works harder to pump blood to the skin for cooling, which raises effective HR and calorie burn. The American College of Sports Medicine estimates a 5–8% increase in metabolic cost in hot conditions.

The effect is U-shaped that means both cold and hot increase burn above a comfortable baseline (roughly 18–22°C / Cloudy conditions).
